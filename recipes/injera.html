Wellcome to the world of injera

Injera is a spongy, tangy flatbread that is a staple in Ethiopian cuisine. Made from teff flour, it is naturally gluten-free and fermented, giving it a unique flavor and texture. Injera is traditionally used as a utensil to scoop up stews and salads, making it an essential part of Ethiopian meals.

Ingridients

  • teff flour
  • water
  • salt
  • baking soda

steps of creating injera

  1. Mix Batter: Combine teff flour, water, and salt in a bowl to form a thin batter. Cover and let sit for 1-3 days to ferment.
  2. Prepare Batter: Stir in baking soda to the fermented batter to create bubbles and a spongy texture.
  3. Cook Injera: Heat a non-stick skillet or injera pan over medium heat. Pour a ladle of batter onto the pan and swirl to spread thinly. Cover and cook until bubbles form and the edges lift.
  4. Serve: Once cooked, remove the injera from the pan and stack on a plate. Serve with your favorite Ethiopian stews and salads.
